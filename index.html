Podcasts

  • "Music, Memory, and Auditory Angels" [19:05] from Sound Matters (documentary)
    Pretty much every important human event is marked by music, and our brains take it all in, no matter how distant or vague those memories become. In this episode we meet Paul Robertson, violinist and professor in music and medicine, who has spent years working with people suffering from dementia and brain damage.
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
  • "The Vietnam Tapes of Michael A. Baronowski" [30:38] from Fugitive Waves (documentary)
    Michael Baronowski was a 19-year-old Marine when he landed in Vietnam in 1966. Before he was killed in action in 1967, he recorded audio letters for his family back in Norristown, Pennsylvania. Produced by Jay Allison and Christina Egloff.
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
  • "Junot Diaz on Intimacy and the Game of Fiction" [1:22:30] from NYPL Podcast (Interview)
    In this conversation with New York Public Library's Paul Holdengraber, Díaz talks about family, love, and the American immigrant experience.
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
  • "Do Not Drink" [53:41] from Reveal (investigation)
    The economically depressed city of Flint, Michigan, is making headlines across the country because there’s something in its water that shouldn't be there.
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
  • "Artificial Intelligence" [43:15] from The Infinite Monkey Cage (comedy)
    The infinite monkeys are joined on stage by comedian Jo Brand, neuroscientist Anil Seth, and robotics expert Alan Winfield to discuss artificial intelligence.
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
  • "Tristan and Iseult" [45:12] from In Our Time (discussion)
    Tristan and Iseult seemed ideally matched and their love was heroic, but could that excuse their adultery, in the minds of medieval listeners, particularly when the Church was so clear they were wrong?
    Listen | Visit website | RSS
